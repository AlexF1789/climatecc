Lieksan Yläkoulu

Lieksan yläkoulu is a secondary school with 229 students, 33 teachers, 3 special needs assistants and other staff. We are an active school in a disadvantageous area. Our pupils are 12-16 years old. Lieksa as a town has suffered declining population, high unemployment and ageing inhabitants. This has not stopped us being an active and hard-working school that has taken actively part in Comenius, Erasmus + and International Karelia projects with National Board of Education. We were also starting a student exchange project with a Chinese school in Dequing until the Korona epidemic put all projects in indefinite hold. We also have students from Russia, Somalia and Ethiopia studying in our school. Students with learning difficulties or language barrier study partly alongside with other students and partly in small special classes. The students’ needs in different areas of learning are taken care of and they are given special care.


Lieksan Lukio

Lieksan lukio is a general upper secondary school (sixth form) in the municipity of Lieksa in eastern Finland. There are altogether 90 students from 16 to 19 years old aiming for eligibility for higher studies and 5 lecturers teaching solely in the sixth form. Some students utilize the collaboration between Lieksan lukio and Riveria, which is the biggest vocational upper secnodary education and training provider in eastern Finland. These students achieve a dual qualification combining general and vocational studies. In the sixth form all students must include at least 75 courses to their studies. The local curricula takes into account vibrant music life manifested by the local lconversatory and Lieksan vaskiviikko, which is the biggest festival of brass music in Nordic countries. The city of Lieksa situates approximately 100 kilometers away from the nearest university, the University of Eastern Finland in Joensuu. As universities and general upper seconary schools collaborate in Finland, students can also study university courses at front. From 2021 the brand new campus opened its doors to some 300 children between 11 to 19 years old and simultaneously bringing together young learners and the stuff from the compulsory education and the sixth form.
