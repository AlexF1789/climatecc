Zespol Szkol Centrum Edukacji im. Ignacego Lukasiewicza

School consists of 2 types-general secondary education and vocational,940 students staff-70 teachers , all staff is paid. Students are 15-19 year old, they learn in classes about 30 students or groups about 16. School provides various types of activities, from sport to art.This is a secondary school located in Płock, a town 100 km from Warsaw, in the central Poland. Płock is situated in the industrial area, but it is a historical town with the rich cultural heritage .Since 2014, our students have participated in the implementation of measures for the prevention of substance abuse among young people,cyberbullying ,violence at school . Group of our students participated in the Presidential Palace in the debate on the occasion of 10 - anniversary of Polish accession to the European Union. Young people interested in the subject of the EU, taking part in competitions organized by Polish MEPs, and as a reward - leaving for Brussels. Technical students over the years not only interested in issues related to their future profession, they also participate in the National Competition "Poland in NATO", Polish Olympic Games Knowledge of the European Union "Stardust Circle", Polish Olympic Games Knowledge of State and Law, occupying leading positions on the provincial stage, and even becoming winners. Young people from our school participated in the project "School of the Future in 2014."Turn on thinking" activity, which aims to prepare our teaching staff for use in the process of teaching the techniques of creative thinking, has been carried out. Our students many years occupy leading positions in the classification of sports high schools in Plock and in urban raids.Working on the international school project can help solve some problems with joining some students actively to school work, after school activities, to join their partners to school projects, to support students' communicative skills through the mobilities. Language knowledge of our students is very good, so they are keen on working on the common topics together with their partners from EU.
