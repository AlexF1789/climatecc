LTTA 2: Water scarcity

It was organised in Barcelona, Spain between 2023 January 16th - 20th.
The main aim of the LTTA was to analyse the causes and consequences of Climate Change in water and in the environment, causing floods, droughts, devastation of fisheries, loss of biodiversity, habitat destruction as well as the change of water quality. In order to do it, students researched about it, collected data, calculated their water footprint, interviewed an activist they had chosen and presented all the information in the Erasmus meeting in Barcelona. They saw their partners' research, discussed the problem of water scarcity in international groups of 6 and looked for real solutions. They organized a campaign to raise awareness in the school, home, neighbourhood and created posters. To complete their research, they visited the desalination and purification plant in Prat de Llobregat where our local TV interviewed the students and they also attend a workshop organized by The Catalan Water Agency. They interviewed experts and created a final video with all the information obtained in the visits, workshops, team work. A video contest was held at the school. All the activities were posted on the school web, on Instagram, Facebook, Twitter and shown on our local television L-H Hospitalet del Llobregat. Thanks to all these activities, the students could see that water scarcity is a problem which affects all the countries and it can be stopped if we work together. The first step was to raise awareness in our school, neighbourhood and families. So with the dissemination activities carried out, all of us are more aware and we have been able to reduce our water consumption at home, at school and in our neighbourhood. We have realised that as Climate Change is an international issue, the solutions have to be taken together and thanks to this project, the students have improved their skills such as critical thinking, problem solving, teamwork, ICT competences and communication in English. Now they are more responsible and more engaged in the school activities. What's more, they feel more mature to participate in environmental events.
